
INDIA TOURISM MEGA GUIDE – ALL 28 STATES (COMPACT TXT VERSION)
==============================================================

NOTE: This file gives a tourism snapshot for EVERY Indian state in a consistent structure:

------------------------------------------------------------
1) ANDHRA PRADESH – COAST, TEMPLES & TIRUPATI
------------------------------------------------------------

[State Snapshot]
Andhra Pradesh combines a long Bay of Bengal coastline, important pilgrimage centres, and scenic hills. Tourists mainly visit Tirupati, Visakhapatnam (Vizag) and Araku Valley.

[Best Time to Visit]
- Best: November–February (pleasant, less humidity).
- Shoulder: July–September (green landscapes, some rain).
- Avoid: April–June (very hot, often 40°C+ inland).

[Top Cities & Regions]
- Tirupati/Tirumala – Major temple town.
- Visakhapatnam – Coastal city with beaches and hills.
- Araku Valley – Coffee‑growing hill area.
- Vijayawada/Amaravati – Riverfront and temple tourism.

[Highlight Themes]
Pilgrimage (Tirupati), beaches (Vizag), hill trips (Araku), coastal food and culture.

------------------------------------------------------------
2) ARUNACHAL PRADESH – MONASTERIES & HIMALAYAN SCENERY
------------------------------------------------------------

[State Snapshot]
A remote and scenic Himalayan state with Buddhist monasteries, high‑altitude passes and tribal culture. Tourism is relatively offbeat and permit‑based in many areas.

[Best Time to Visit]
- Best: October–April (cool, clearer skies in most hill regions).
- Avoid: June–September (heavy monsoon, landslide risk).

[Top Cities & Regions]
- Tawang – Monastery town near Bhutan border.
- Bomdila/Dirang – Hill towns en route to Tawang.
- Ziro Valley – Paddy fields and Apatani tribal villages.
- Itanagar – Capital and administrative hub.

[Highlight Themes]
Offbeat mountain travel, monasteries, tribal culture, scenic roads.

------------------------------------------------------------
3) ASSAM – TEA GARDENS, RHINOS & BRAHMAPUTRA
------------------------------------------------------------

[State Snapshot]
Assam sits along the Brahmaputra River with tea estates, wildlife reserves and river islands. It’s the gateway to much of Northeast India.

[Best Time to Visit]
- Best: November–April (pleasant, national parks open).
- Avoid: June–September (monsoon, floods in some areas).

[Top Cities & Regions]
- Guwahati – Main city, Kamakhya Temple, river cruises.
- Kaziranga – National park for one‑horned rhinos.
- Majuli – Large river island with monasteries (satras).
- Jorhat/Dibrugarh – Tea towns.

[Highlight Themes]
Wildlife safaris, river tourism, pilgrimage, tea estate experiences.

------------------------------------------------------------
4) BIHAR – BUDDHIST SITES & ANCIENT UNIVERSITIES
------------------------------------------------------------

[State Snapshot]
Historically important for Buddhism and ancient learning. Key hubs include Bodh Gaya, Nalanda and Rajgir, plus the capital Patna.

[Best Time to Visit]
- Best: November–February (cool and comfortable).
- Shoulder: October & March.
- Avoid: April–June (very hot), heavy monsoon months.

[Top Cities & Regions]
- Bodh Gaya – Mahabodhi Temple and global Buddhist pilgrimage.
- Nalanda/Rajgir – Ruins and hills connected to Buddha.
- Patna – Capital city on the Ganga River.

[Highlight Themes]
Buddhist circuit tourism, ancient history, spiritual retreats.

------------------------------------------------------------
5) CHHATTISGARH – WATERFALLS & TRIBAL HEARTLAND
------------------------------------------------------------

[State Snapshot]
A largely forested state with waterfalls, caves and tribal regions, especially around Bastar and Jagdalpur. Less commercialised than many other states.

[Best Time to Visit]
- Best: October–February (cool, ideal for outdoor trips).
- Avoid: Peak summer (April–June) and heavy monsoon for remote travel.

[Top Cities & Regions]
- Raipur – Capital and transit hub.
- Jagdalpur/Bastar – Waterfalls, caves and tribal culture.
- Chitrakote & Tirathgarh – Major waterfalls.

[Highlight Themes]
Eco‑tourism, tribal culture, waterfalls and caves.

------------------------------------------------------------
6) GOA – BEACHES, NIGHTLIFE & PORTUGUESE HERITAGE
------------------------------------------------------------

[State Snapshot]
India’s beach and party hotspot with a strong Portuguese influence, divided into livelier North Goa and quieter South Goa.

[Best Time to Visit]
- Peak: November–February (dry, pleasant, big events).
- Shoulder: October & March.
- Monsoon: June–September (lush, cheaper, fewer water sports).

[Top Cities & Regions]
- North Goa – Baga, Calangute, Candolim, Anjuna, Vagator.
- South Goa – Palolem, Agonda, Colva, Varca.
- Panaji (Panjim) – Latin Quarter and central base.

[Highlight Themes]
Beaches, nightlife, seafood, heritage churches and forts.

------------------------------------------------------------
7) GUJARAT – WHITE DESERT, LIONS & COASTAL TEMPLES
------------------------------------------------------------

[State Snapshot]
Gujarat offers the Great Rann of Kutch, Asiatic lions in Gir, vibrant textile crafts and important Hindu temples on the coast.

[Best Time to Visit]
- Best: November–February (pleasant, Rann Utsav).
- Shoulder: October & March.
- Avoid: Peak summer and monsoon for desert and wildlife travel.

[Top Cities & Regions]
- Ahmedabad – Heritage city and gateway.
- Bhuj/Kutch – White desert and craft villages.
- Sasan Gir – National park for lions.
- Dwarka/Somnath – Coastal temple towns.

[Highlight Themes]
Desert landscapes, wildlife safaris, pilgrimage, handicrafts.

------------------------------------------------------------
8) HARYANA – URBAN HUBS & MAHABHARATA HERITAGE
------------------------------------------------------------

[State Snapshot]
Surrounds Delhi with fast‑growing cities like Gurugram and historically important Kurukshetra. Popular more for business than leisure.

[Best Time to Visit]
- Best: October–March (pleasant winter).
- Avoid: May–June (intense summer heat).

[Top Cities & Regions]
- Gurugram (Gurgaon) – Business, malls, nightlife.
- Faridabad/Panipat – Industrial and historical towns.
- Kurukshetra – Mythological battlefield of the Mahabharata.

[Highlight Themes]
Business travel, shopping, mythological and religious tourism.

------------------------------------------------------------
9) HIMACHAL PRADESH – HILL STATIONS & SNOWY VALLEYS
------------------------------------------------------------

[State Snapshot]
Classic north Indian hill state with famous towns like Shimla, Manali, Dharamshala and offbeat valleys like Spiti and Kinnaur.

[Best Time to Visit]
- Hills (Shimla/Manali): March–June, September–November.
- Snow: December–February (higher altitudes).
- Spiti: Roughly June–September (roads open).

[Top Cities & Regions]
- Shimla – Former British summer capital.
- Manali & Solang – Hills, adventure, snow.
- Dharamshala/McLeod Ganj – Tibetan monasteries and cafes.
- Spiti/Kinnaur – High‑altitude, offbeat road trips.

[Highlight Themes]
Hill vacations, snow trips, trekking, monasteries and mountain road journeys.

------------------------------------------------------------
10) JHARKHAND – FALLS, FORESTS & PLATEAU VIEWS
------------------------------------------------------------

[State Snapshot]
A plateau state with waterfalls, forests and mining towns. Tourism is still developing but offers scenic day trips, especially around Ranchi.

[Best Time to Visit]
- Best: October–February (cool, pleasant).
- Avoid: April–June (hot), heavy monsoon for outdoor waterfalls.

[Top Cities & Regions]
- Ranchi – Capital, gateway to nearby falls.
- Netarhat – Hill station.
- Betla – National park and forest area.

[Highlight Themes]
Waterfalls, quiet hill getaways, forest drives.

------------------------------------------------------------
11) KARNATAKA – RUINS, COFFEE HILLS & TECH CITY
------------------------------------------------------------

[State Snapshot]
Home to tech hub Bengaluru, royal Mysuru, UNESCO‑listed Hampi ruins, coffee estates in Coorg/Chikmagalur and coastal Gokarna.

[Best Time to Visit]
- Best: October–March for most regions.
- Hills: Also scenic in monsoon if you don’t mind rain.
- Avoid: Very hot months in interior plains.

[Top Cities & Regions]
- Bengaluru – IT city with cafes and nightlife.
- Mysuru – Palace, markets, Dasara festival.
- Hampi – Ancient ruins and boulderscapes.
- Coorg/Chikmagalur – Coffee hills and homestays.
- Gokarna – Quieter beaches.

[Highlight Themes]
Heritage, hills, coffee tourism, beaches and city breaks.

------------------------------------------------------------
12) KERALA – BACKWATERS, GREEN HILLS & WELLNESS
------------------------------------------------------------

[State Snapshot]
“God’s Own Country” with palm‑lined backwaters, tea gardens, beaches and Ayurvedic retreats.

[Best Time to Visit]
- Best: September–March (pleasant weather).
- Monsoon: June–August (lush and ideal for Ayurveda).
- Avoid: Peak summer humidity if you’re heat‑sensitive.

[Top Cities & Regions]
- Kochi/Fort Kochi – Historic port and art hub.
- Munnar – Tea‑covered hills.
- Alleppey/Kumarakom – Backwater houseboats and resorts.
- Kovalam/Varkala – Beaches and cliff views.
- Wayanad – Forested hills and wildlife.

[Highlight Themes]
Backwater cruises, hill stations, beach stays, Ayurveda and cultural experiences.

------------------------------------------------------------
13) MADHYA PRADESH – TEMPLES, CAVES & TIGER RESERVES
------------------------------------------------------------

[State Snapshot]
Central Indian state often called “Heart of India”, with Khajuraho temples, forts and major national parks like Kanha and Bandhavgarh.

[Best Time to Visit]
- Wildlife: November–April (parks usually shut during heavy monsoon).
- Heritage: October–March (avoid extreme summer).

[Top Cities & Regions]
- Khajuraho – Carved temples, UNESCO site.
- Bhopal – Lakes, gateway to Sanchi/Bhimbetka.
- Gwalior/Orchha – Forts and palaces.
- Kanha/Bandhavgarh/Pench – Tiger reserves.

[Highlight Themes]
Temple architecture, rock art, tiger safaris and historic forts.

------------------------------------------------------------
14) MAHARASHTRA – MEGACITY, CAVES & WESTERN GHATS
------------------------------------------------------------

[State Snapshot]
Large and diverse state with Mumbai, Pune, ancient rock‑cut caves, wine regions and Western Ghats hill stations.

[Best Time to Visit]
- Cities: October–March.
- Hills: Monsoon (lush) and winter.
- Avoid: Extreme summer heat in interiors.

[Top Cities & Regions]
- Mumbai – Financial and film capital on the coast.
- Pune – Cultural and educational hub.
- Aurangabad – Gateway to Ajanta & Ellora caves.
- Lonavala/Mahabaleshwar/Matheran – Popular hill retreats.

[Highlight Themes]
Urban tourism, heritage caves, monsoon hill trips and coastal food.

------------------------------------------------------------
15) MANIPUR – FLOATING LAKE & HILL CULTURE
------------------------------------------------------------

[State Snapshot]
Northeastern state known for Loktak Lake’s floating islands, classical dance forms and historic Imphal.

[Best Time to Visit]
- Best: October–March (cool and mostly dry).
- Avoid: Heavy monsoon months.

[Top Cities & Regions]
- Imphal – Capital with markets and memorials.
- Loktak Lake – Floating phumdis and lake stays.
- Moirang – Historical and cultural sites near the lake.

[Highlight Themes]
Lake tourism, culture, nature and quieter hill life.

------------------------------------------------------------
16) MEGHALAYA – CLOUDS, CAVES & LIVING ROOT BRIDGES
------------------------------------------------------------

[State Snapshot]
Meghalaya offers lush hills, high rainfall zones, waterfalls and famous living root bridges in Khasi villages.

[Best Time to Visit]
- Best: October–April (good visibility and pleasant).
- Monsoon: Very heavy rain; scenic but can disrupt travel.

[Top Cities & Regions]
- Shillong – Capital and hill town with cafes.
- Cherrapunji/Sohra – Waterfalls, root bridges, viewpoints.
- Dawki – Crystal‑clear river on the Bangladesh border.

[Highlight Themes]
Waterfalls, trekking, caves, village tourism and scenic drives.

------------------------------------------------------------
17) MIZORAM – QUIET HILLS & BAMBOO FORESTS
------------------------------------------------------------

[State Snapshot]
A peaceful, hilly state with winding roads, churches on ridges and bamboo‑covered slopes; tourism is small‑scale and community‑based.

[Best Time to Visit]
- Best: October–March (cool and comfortable).
- Avoid: Peak monsoon for rural road travel.

[Top Cities & Regions]
- Aizawl – Hilltop capital.
- Lunglei – Scenic viewpoints and countryside.
- Champhai belt – Vineyards and border landscapes.

[Highlight Themes]
Slow hill travel, church architecture, nature and village life.

------------------------------------------------------------
18) NAGALAND – TRIBAL FESTIVALS & HILL VILLAGES
------------------------------------------------------------

[State Snapshot]
Nagaland is known for its many tribes, traditional villages and the Hornbill Festival, celebrated near Kohima.

[Best Time to Visit]
- Best: October–March (especially early December for Hornbill).
- Avoid: Heavy monsoon months.

[Top Cities & Regions]
- Kohima – Capital, war cemetery and festival ground.
- Dimapur – Main gateway city.
- Heritage villages (e.g., Khonoma) – Traditional Angami village.

[Highlight Themes]
Tribal culture, festivals, hill village stays and handicrafts.

------------------------------------------------------------
19) ODISHA – TEMPLES, BEACHES & LAGOONS
------------------------------------------------------------

[State Snapshot]
Odisha has famous temples, long beaches and Asia’s largest brackish lagoon, Chilika Lake.

[Best Time to Visit]
- Best: October–February (cool and pleasant).
- Avoid: Intense summer heat and peak cyclone periods on the coast.

[Top Cities & Regions]
- Bhubaneswar – Temple city and capital.
- Puri – Jagannath Temple and beach.
- Konark – Sun Temple.
- Chilika Lake – Birding and boat trips.

[Highlight Themes]
Temple tourism, coastal trips, lake and birdwatching experiences.

------------------------------------------------------------
20) PUNJAB – GOLDEN TEMPLE & PUNJABI CULTURE
------------------------------------------------------------

[State Snapshot]
Known for fertile fields, bhangra, hearty food and the Golden Temple in Amritsar, plus the India–Pakistan border ceremony at Wagah/Attari.

[Best Time to Visit]
- Best: October–March (pleasant winter).
- Avoid: Peak summer (very hot) for daytime sightseeing.

[Top Cities & Regions]
- Amritsar – Golden Temple, Jallianwala Bagh, border ceremony.
- Chandigarh (shared capital with Haryana) – Planned city.
- Ludhiana/Patiala – Urban centres with local culture.

[Highlight Themes]
Religious tourism, patriotic sites, Punjabi food and hospitality.

------------------------------------------------------------
21) RAJASTHAN – FORTS, PALACES & THAR DESERT
------------------------------------------------------------

[State Snapshot]
Royal desert state with colourful cities, massive forts, havelis and sand dunes. One of India’s classic tourism circuits.

[Best Time to Visit]
- Best: October–March (cool desert winter).
- Avoid: May–June (scorching heat).

[Top Cities & Regions]
- Jaipur – “Pink City” with forts and palaces.
- Udaipur – Lakes and romantic palaces.
- Jodhpur – Blue city and Mehrangarh Fort.
- Jaisalmer – Golden fort and Thar desert dunes.
- Pushkar – Lake, temples and fair.

[Highlight Themes]
Heritage stays, camel safaris, forts, palaces, colourful markets.

------------------------------------------------------------
22) SIKKIM – SNOW PEAKS & MONASTERIES
------------------------------------------------------------

[State Snapshot]
A small Himalayan state with Gangtok, high passes, alpine lakes and Buddhist monasteries.

[Best Time to Visit]
- Best: March–June and September–November.
- Avoid: Peak monsoon and heavy winter snows on certain routes.

[Top Cities & Regions]
- Gangtok – Capital, viewpoints and cable car.
- North Sikkim (Lachung, Lachen) – High mountains and lakes.
- Pelling – Views of Kanchenjunga and monasteries.

[Highlight Themes]
Himalayan landscapes, monasteries, mild adventure and scenic drives.

------------------------------------------------------------
23) TAMIL NADU – TEMPLES, HERITAGE TOWNS & HILL STATIONS
------------------------------------------------------------

[State Snapshot]
A major South Indian state with grand Dravidian temples, historic coastal towns and hill stations like Ooty and Kodaikanal.

[Best Time to Visit]
- Best: November–February for the plains and temples.
- Hills: Pleasant almost year‑round, cooler in summer.
- Avoid: Hottest months in interior plains.

[Top Cities & Regions]
- Chennai – Coastal metropolis and gateway.
- Madurai – Meenakshi Temple.
- Thanjavur/Kumbakonam belt – Temple towns.
- Ooty/Kodaikanal – Hill stations.
- Rameswaram/Kanyakumari – Sacred and scenic coastal ends.

[Highlight Themes]
Temple architecture, coastal culture, hill retreats and classical arts.

------------------------------------------------------------
24) TELANGANA – NIZAMI HERITAGE & MODERN TECH CITY
------------------------------------------------------------

[State Snapshot]
Newer state formed from Andhra Pradesh, with Hyderabad as a major IT and cultural hub known for biryani and old city monuments.

[Best Time to Visit]
- Best: October–February (cooler, pleasant).
- Avoid: Very hot summer months.

[Top Cities & Regions]
- Hyderabad – Charminar, Golconda Fort, Hussain Sagar, IT corridors.
- Warangal – Forts and historic temples.
- Nizamabad/Karimnagar – Smaller cultural centres.

[Highlight Themes]
City tourism, Nizami heritage, food trails and IT/business travel.

------------------------------------------------------------
25) TRIPURA – PALACES & ROCK CARVINGS
------------------------------------------------------------

[State Snapshot]
A small northeastern state with royal palaces, lakes and historic rock carvings, quieter than many tourist circuits.

[Best Time to Visit]
- Best: October–March (pleasant).
- Avoid: Intense monsoon months.

[Top Cities & Regions]
- Agartala – Capital and Ujjayanta Palace.
- Udaipur (Tripura) – Lakes and temples.
- Unakoti – Rock‑cut sculptures.

[Highlight Themes]
Heritage palaces, rock art, lakes and low‑key travel.

------------------------------------------------------------
26) UTTAR PRADESH – TAJ MAHAL, HOLY TOWNS & PLAINS
------------------------------------------------------------

[State Snapshot]
India’s most populous state with huge religious importance and major monuments, including the Taj Mahal and Ganga ghats.

[Best Time to Visit]
- Best: October–March (manageable temperatures).
- Avoid: Peak summer and peak monsoon for intense heat/humidity.

[Top Cities & Regions]
- Agra – Taj Mahal, Agra Fort, nearby Fatehpur Sikri.
- Varanasi – Ganga ghats and spiritual rituals.
- Ayodhya – Ram temple town.
- Lucknow – Nawabi heritage and food.
- Mathura/Varanadavan – Krishna circuit.

[Highlight Themes]
Heritage monuments, religious tourism, Nawabi cuisine and cultural festivals.

------------------------------------------------------------
27) UTTARAKHAND – YOGA TOWNS & HIMALAYAN SHRINES
------------------------------------------------------------

[State Snapshot]
Mountainous state with yoga hubs on the Ganga, pilgrimage circuits and hill stations like Nainital and Mussoorie.

[Best Time to Visit]
- Hills: March–June, September–November.
- Char Dham Yatra: Late spring to early autumn (seasonal).
- Avoid: Peak monsoon for landslide‑prone areas.

[Top Cities & Regions]
- Rishikesh/Haridwar – Yoga, Ganga Aarti, spiritual stays.
- Nainital – Lake hill station.
- Mussoorie – Popular hill town.
- Char Dham (Kedarnath, Badrinath etc.) – High‑altitude pilgrimage.

[Highlight Themes]
Spiritual retreats, trekking, hill holidays and pilgrimage.

------------------------------------------------------------
28) WEST BENGAL – KOLKATA, DARJEELING & SUNDARBANS
------------------------------------------------------------

[State Snapshot]
Eastern state with Kolkata’s colonial heritage, Darjeeling’s tea hills and the Sundarbans mangrove delta.

[Best Time to Visit]
- Kolkata plains: November–February.
- Hills (Darjeeling/Kalimpong): March–June, September–November.
- Avoid: Heavy monsoon for delta and hill travel.

[Top Cities & Regions]
- Kolkata – Culture, food, colonial streets, Durga Puja.
- Darjeeling – Tea gardens and toy train.
- Kalimpong – Quieter hill town.
- Sundarbans – Mangrove forests and boat safaris.

[Highlight Themes]
City culture, tea tourism, mangrove eco‑tourism and festivals.
